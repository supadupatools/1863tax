1863 TAX
ASSESSMENT

NC • 1863

The 1863 tax assessment is critical because enslaved people were often assessed as taxable property and, in some counties, recorded by name in ways missing from other records.

For Black American researchers, those entries can be one of the few direct paper trails connecting individuals, households, enslavers, and place before emancipation.

Surviving 1863 tax rolls in North Carolina are limited, with only a small set of counties, roughly seven to eight, known to retain usable records.

That scarcity makes every named entry high-impact evidence for reconstructing Black family lines, migration, kinship ties, and community history.
