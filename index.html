NC • 1863

The 1863 tax assessment is critical because enslaved people were often assessed as taxable property and, in some counties, recorded by name in ways missing from other records.

Surviving North Carolina rolls are scarce. That scarcity makes each named entry high-impact evidence for reconstructing Black family lines, migration, and community history.
